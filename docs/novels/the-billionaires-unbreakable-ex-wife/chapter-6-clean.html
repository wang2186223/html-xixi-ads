Chapter 6

"I'm sorry." Judith stumbled back repeatedly, her face etched with terror.

Ella could never forget the fleeting look of provocation that had crossed her face moments before; she knew it was all a facade.

All around them, countless eyes turned in their direction, unwavering and piercing. Whispers began to ripple through the crowd. weaving a tapestry of murmured speculation around the two women.

"Did you see her with Mr. Raymond? I bet she's trying to seduce him." A woman whispered.

"Ms. Brooks should dump a glass of red wine on her. Teach her a lesson."another added.

"Why is Ms. Brooks being so nice? She shouldn't have apologized to her at all."

Ella heard the venomous comments directed at her.

Every cruel word landed like a slap.

The ironywas suffocating. Judith had deliberately spilt wine on her dress.yet somehow Ella had become the villain in everyone's eyes.

Seething with fury, Ella glanced down at the glaring crimson stain marring her gilded gown, her fingers tightening subtly.

Before she could utter a word, Judith preempted her, assuming a pitiful demeanor as if wronged. "Austin, you must explain to Ella; please tell her not to be angry with me!"

She gently tugged at Austin's sleeve, casting a timid glance at Ella.

Austin parted his lips, his voice icy, "Judith didn't do it deliberately; don't direct your anger at her."

Since when had she ever targeted Judith? Had she uttered a single word against her all along?

Bereft of patience, Ella's outrage boiled over. "Do you expect me to pretend that everyone around here is blind?"

Her original intent had been to remind Austin of the witnesses nearby, not anticipating that Judith would break down into tears first.

"You mean I deliberately doused you in wine? Ella, how could you think so ill of me? I truly didn't mean it..."

Judith hid her face, weeping softly as if grievously wronged; those unaware might believe she was the victim.

Austin's brow furrowed sharply as he watched her tears, his gaze cutting coldly. "Ella, have you caused enough trouble?"

Though prepared for this, seeing Austin side with Judith filled Ella with a dull pain deep in her heart.

She fixed Austin with a frosty stare, brimming with alienation and disappointment sharp enough to wound.

His lips pressed into a thin line, his tone stern, "Ella, stop pretending to be the aggrieved party. No one here owes you anything."

His heart unequivocally leaned toward Judith.

Whenever Ella sought to draw a line, it was dismissed as mere sensibility

or melodrama.

Clutching the hem of her dress, fingertips digging into her palm.Ella's voice was fierce, "If an apology is what you want, make it sincere. This dress is ruined; you should compensate me with a new one."

The crowd erupted.

"How dare she extort over a torn dress?"

"Looks like a cheap knockoff masquerading as a designer piece, probably not worth as much as that bottle of wine."

"Poor Judith is too kind to be bothered by such a person."

"She's just pushing her luck, even after the apology."

"How shameless, clinging onto Judith like that?"

The onlookers, ignorant of the facts, erupted in disdainful whispers.

Even Austin's expression darkened with displeasure.

"Ella, don't be unreasonable." His voice was grave, his gaze sharp and unsparing. "Judith has apologized; don't press the matter further."

Unyielding, Ella met his eyes without flinching. "So, her apology is so precious that a single 'sorry' absolves her of all sins?"

Austin's fury deepened as her defiance persisted, about to erupt, when a voice suddenly broke through with surprise.

"Miss Brooks, what a coincidence to see you here!"

Following the voice. Ella was taken aback to find an old acquaintance of

her grandfather,Gerald Clark.

She had been the true heiress, but lost at three years old and taken in by her reclusive grandfather in the countryside. Her mother. Janice Clark,died of grief after fruitless searches.

Her father. John Brooks, burdened by her mother's death,brought along his mistress, Carol Baker, and Judith, two years older, establishing a legitimate marriage.

Her grandmother suffered a stroke from the shock and never recovered.

At last, when she was ten, her grandfather formally brought her home.

Yet fate had it that Judith grew into a prodigious designer, revered and embraced as the legitimate Brooks family heiress, while Ella remained unknown.

Her husband was entwined with her half-sister in a seemingly perfect union.

She never revealed her true origin; thus, everyone sought to trample her beneath their feet.

Ella nodded silently in acknowledgment.

Beside her, Judith smiled brightly as she approached."Mr. Bales, Mr.Dobbins, my dad has mentioned you many times; I didn't expect you to be at this banquet..."

They exchanged polite, cursory nods.

Judith inwardly rejoiced, her smile blossoming even wider.

Daron Bales was the magnate behind Summit Technologies Group.

Valentin Dobbins was the chief researcher and investor of the Alpha Project. Both were titans in the scientifie and investment spheres.

She had once met Daron on an outing with her father, making a fleeting acquaintance.

This was her first time meeting Valentin, though she had heard rumors and yearned to forge a connection.

Now, unexpectedly encountering them here was a perfect opportunity to strengthen ties and covertly undermine Ella, truly killing two birds with one stone.

As predicted, the crowd erupted once more.

"Mr. Bales, is that the renowned head of Summit Technologies Group?"

"Rumor has it that he and Mr. Dobbins jointly launched the Alpha Project,with investment from the Clark family. No wonder Judith is so close to them."

"How enviable for Judith to have such a doting grandfather."

Judith raised her crimson lips, basking in the adulation. Ella's heart brimmed with disdain.

Judith had never once visited their gravely ill grandmother during mourning,yet now she was their grandfather's favored granddaughter.

Having long avoided conflict and endured silently, today,Ella resolved to no longer restrain herself.

Stepping forward, her lips parted softly, "Mr. Bales, Mr. Dobbins, are you here to discuss the Alpha Project?"

Daron glanced at Judith before smiling politely at Ella. "Your grandfather's health has declined, preventing him from liaising with us. We worried about potential setbacks and have been seeking someone to coordinate."

Volunteering eagerly, Ella declared, "I have long followed the Alpha Project and compiled considerable research materials. If you place your trust in me, I would be honored to assist."

"Ella, the Alpha Project is critical Al research. Don't burden Mr. Bales with trouble." Judith urged anxiously, her expression tinged with unease.

"I know. I once said I regarded you as a sister, but that doesn't grant you the right to truly claim the Brooks family name. The Alpha Project is of

utmost importance, and..." Judith paused for effect, laden with implication.

"You aren't well acquainted with Mr. Bales. What if you offend him..."

She trailed off, leaving room for the crowd's imagination.

Feeling the weight of scornful gazes from every direction, Ella's nails dug mercilessly into her palms.

Just as she prepared to respond, Daron's curious eyes fixed on her. "Miss.as far as I recall, Mr. John Brooks has but one granddaughter, Ella.May I ask who you are?"

Feigning ignorance, Daron's candid words caused Judith's face to flush scarlet instantly. "Mr. Bales, I'm Judith! We've met before!"

Frowning, Daron pretended to deliberate, then shook his head.

"Apologies, I seem to have no recollection."

Before Judith could insist on her identity, his gaze shifted to Ella's soiled gown. "That is a limited edition gown. What a shame it's been stained."

Judith's complexion transformed abruptly.

What? Limited edition?
